Campaign Messaging Guidelines

The switching angle

Every campaign asset should make switching to Rentvine feel like the obvious, low-risk choice. We lead with the pain of staying, then prove the ease of moving. The message is not "Rentvine is great" — it's "your current software is costing you, and switching is easier than you think."

"42 days to go live. 80% of migration handled for you. Month-end close in hours, not days."
"The #1-rated property management platform. Your competitors are already there."

Voice guidelines

Rentvine copy should feel confident, direct, and human. We don't hedge. We don't over-explain. We write for the PM who has 40 tabs open and 12 minutes for lunch.

ToneConfident without being cocky. Helpful without being soft.
Sentence lengthShort. If it takes two sentences, make it one.
Always lead withThe pain or problem, not the feature.
Never say"Robust," "seamless," "cutting-edge," "all-in-one solution."
NumbersUse specific proof points. "Hours, not days" beats "faster."
CTA style"See how it works" over "Request a demo." Active, low-friction.

Product Roadmap

Product's role in the campaign

Product features are not the story — they are the proof. Every feature we highlight exists to answer one customer question: "What makes switching worth it?" We don't lead with what the feature does. We lead with what it solves for a property manager who has been burned before.

The switching campaign works when product and marketing move together: product ships the thing, marketing turns it into evidence. Fixie AI is proof that AI actually saves time. Month-end close is proof that accounting doesn't have to be painful. The Mobile App is proof that Rentvine works how PMs actually work — on the go.
